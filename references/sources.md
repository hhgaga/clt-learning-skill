# Sources

Primary research papers underlying this skill. The skill's claims should be traceable to these.

---

## Foundational

**Sweller, J. (1988).** *Cognitive Load During Problem Solving: Effects on Learning.* Cognitive Science, 12, 257–285.
- The origin paper. Establishes that conventional problem solving via means-ends analysis depletes WM and competes with schema acquisition. Introduces the worked-example effect (with G. Cooper) and the goal-free effect (with M. Levine 1982 maze problems).

**Sweller, J., van Merriënboer, J. J. G., & Paas, F. G. W. C. (1998).** *Cognitive Architecture and Instructional Design.* Educational Psychology Review, 10(3), 251–296.
- The canonical CLT paper. Lays out the cognitive architecture (WM, LTM, schemas, automation), defines intrinsic / extraneous / germane load (germane introduced here for the first time), and surveys the major effects: goal-free, worked example, completion problem, split-attention, modality, redundancy, variability.

---

## Mid-period synthesis

**van Merriënboer, J. J. G., & Sweller, J. (2005).** *Cognitive Load Theory and Complex Learning: Recent Developments and Future Directions.* Educational Psychology Review, 17(2), 147–177.
- Extends CLT from short laboratory experiments to complex, long-duration learning. Introduces the expertise reversal effect explicitly, the guidance-fading / completion strategy, the use of mental effort + performance to compute *instructional efficiency*, and the rapid-assessment test for tracking expertise. Also strengthens cognitive-architecture argument via biological-evolution analogy.

---

## Critical perspective

**de Jong, T. (2010).** *Cognitive load theory, educational research, and instructional design: some food for thought.* Instructional Science, 38, 105–134.
- The most-cited critique. Argues that the three load types are conceptually unclear and hard to distinguish empirically; that the dominant measurement tool (Paas's 9-point rating scale) is weak; that CLT's high-level recommendations overlap with older instructional-design literature; and that the theory in its current form is hard to falsify. Proposes a research agenda for tighter measurement and process-level investigation. **Required reading for any honest CLT skill.**

---

## Modern synthesis

**Paas, F., & van Merriënboer, J. J. G. (2020).** *Cognitive-Load Theory: Methods to Manage Working Memory Load in the Learning of Complex Tasks.* Current Directions in Psychological Science, 1–5.
- Recent compact synthesis. Reframes load management around three loci: the **task**, the **learner**, and the **environment**. Adds methods beyond pure instructional design: collective working memory (collaboration), gesturing/embodiment, motivational and emotional design, attention-capturing-stimuli reduction, eye closure, and stress-suppressing activities (expressive writing). These newer methods are what `learner-side-strategies.md` covers.

## Adjacent / supporting (not in this workspace, but referenced)

These appear in the citations of the five primary papers and inform several specific claims:

- **Miller, G. A. (1956).** *The magical number seven, plus or minus two.* Psychological Review, 63, 81–97. — original WM capacity finding.
- **Cowan, N. (2001).** *The magical number 4 in short-term memory.* Behavioral and Brain Sciences, 24, 87–114. — refined WM estimate (~4 elements).
- **Tarmizi, R., & Sweller, J. (1988).** Demonstrated split-attention effect in geometry instruction.
- **Mousavi, S., Low, R., & Sweller, J. (1995).** Demonstrated modality effect.
- **Chandler, P., & Sweller, J. (1991).** Demonstrated redundancy effect.
- **Pollock, E., Chandler, P., & Sweller, J. (2002).** Demonstrated isolated-elements-first sequencing for high intrinsic load.
- **Kalyuga, S., Ayres, P., Chandler, P., & Sweller, J. (2003).** *The expertise reversal effect.* Educational Psychologist, 38, 23–32.
- **Renkl, A. (1997).** Self-explanation effect with worked examples.
- **van Merriënboer, J. J. G., & Kirschner, P. A. (2018).** *Ten Steps to Complex Learning* (3rd ed.). Routledge. — operational extension (4C/ID model).
