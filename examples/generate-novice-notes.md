# Example — GENERATE: novice study notes for "muscle lever systems"

**User prompt:** *"Make beginner study notes on first/second/third-class lever systems in the human body."*

**Skill output:**

---

**Assumed learner level:** novice (no prior biomechanics schemas).
**Intrinsic load:** high — three categories with shared but rearranged components (fulcrum, effort, load).
**Effects applied:** worked-example, isolated-elements (sequenced), integrated split-attention, signaling, redundancy elimination.

---

### 1. The three pieces (isolated elements first)

Before classes, learn just three labels:
- **Fulcrum (F)** — pivot point. In the body: a joint.
- **Effort (E)** — force you apply. In the body: a muscle pull.
- **Load (L)** — resistance. In the body: bone weight + whatever you're holding.

> Stop here. Re-read until you can recite F / E / L from memory. Schema for the *parts* must be solid before you study the *arrangements*.

### 2. The three classes (now interacting elements)

Class is named by **which piece is in the middle**.

```
Class 1:   E — F — L     (fulcrum in middle)        e.g. nodding the head (atlanto-occipital joint)
Class 2:   F — L — E     (load in middle)           e.g. standing on tiptoes (ball of foot = F, body weight = L, calf muscle = E)
Class 3:   F — E — L     (effort in middle)         e.g. biceps curl (elbow = F, biceps = E, forearm/dumbbell = L)
```

Most muscles in the body are **class 3**. That is the one to over-learn.

### 3. One worked example (biceps curl, class 3)

Step-by-step. Do not skim — read each line, then look back at the diagram above.

1. Identify the joint → elbow. That's **F**.
2. Identify the muscle pulling → biceps inserts on the radius, ~5 cm distal to the elbow. That's **E**.
3. Identify the load → forearm + dumbbell, center of mass ~30 cm from the elbow. That's **L**.
4. Order along the bone: **F (elbow) — E (biceps insertion) — L (hand)**.
5. Effort in the middle ⇒ class 3.

### 4. Self-check (one item only — guidance fades next round)

A patient does a calf raise. Which class? Identify F, E, L by location, then name the class. Don't read on until you've written an answer.

> Answer: F = ball of foot, L = body weight at ankle, E = calf muscle at heel. Order along the foot from toes to heel: F — L — E. Class 2.

### 5. What comes next (do NOT do now)

After this is automatic, the next session adds **mechanical advantage** (E·distance vs L·distance). That layer is intentionally withheld — adding it now would push past working-memory capacity.
